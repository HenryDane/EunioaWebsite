About Us

Eunoia was founded in July 2016 by a high school junior Grace Choi in Sacramento, CA. She started with a vision inspired by James 1:27 and decided to fulfill her longtime dream to start a non-profit that would benefit less fortunate teens in the Sacramento area. Her goal and prayer is that the organization grows from just being in the Sacramento area to being a nationwide organization that makes a big difference for those who need it most.

The non-profit has two parts: The first part is people in the community helping less fortunate teens and homeless youth. The second part is teens helping those in need in the community through community service.

Our Mission:

Our motto is: help teens, impact lives.

Eunoia strives to make the most difference in the local community it serves by helping less fortunate teens, and by helping other teens give back to the community.

We help local teens in the area through various means, including with meals, school supplies, etc.

Eunoia's purpose is to address, educate, coordinate, and provide aid and relief to unfortunate teenagers in local communities across the US and to also assist other teenagers in making a difference by benefiting local community and charitable needs through means that include volunteering and other acts of service.

We provide clothes, food, school supplies, and other necessary essential supplies to teens in the area through reaching out in various ways, through schools, libraries, and other public venues as well as utilizing social media channels and the corporation's website to provide facts, statistics, and other related data on causes, current efforts and solutions to eradicating poverty and other issues that local teens struggle with. At the same time we fundraise and raise awareness for the biggest problems that teens face in today's society.

At the same time our programs include helping other teenagers help out in the community by volunteering and participating in acts of service that will benefit the community and other charitable needs.
